# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Click Battle is for friends playing casually on PC and phone, plus competitive players who care about leaderboard position and personal bests. Users are usually in a quick-play context: they want to create or join a room, invite friends, understand the current mode, play fast rounds, and see results without setup friction.

## Product Purpose

Click Battle exists to make short multiplayer skill battles feel instant, social, and satisfying. Success means players can get into a room quickly, understand what to do at every moment, feel the tactile payoff of each click or reaction, and trust the result enough to play again or chase the leaderboard.

## Brand Personality

Fun, close, friendly, accessible, familiar, satisfying, and adrenaline-driven. The interface should feel like a casual challenge between friends: warm enough for first-time players, responsive enough for competitive players, and energetic without becoming stressful.

## Anti-references

Do not make Click Battle feel corporate, casino-like, gambling-coded, overly childish, hardcore esports, generic SaaS, or visually noisy. Avoid sterile productivity aesthetics, predatory urgency patterns, confusing decorative effects, and interfaces that bury the quick social play loop under unnecessary ceremony.

## Design Principles

- Make the next action obvious: create, join, start, click, react, and replay should always be visually and cognitively clear.
- Preserve tactile satisfaction: primary controls should feel pressable, responsive, and rewarding without slowing players down.
- Balance friendliness with competition: social warmth should coexist with clear rankings, winners, and performance feedback.
- Keep rounds lightweight: minimize setup, explanation, and waiting so the product stays suited to quick breaks and spontaneous friend challenges.
- Communicate state with redundancy: game mode, timing, waiting, false starts, winners, and errors should not rely on color alone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use a practical minimum-accessibility baseline: keyboard-accessible critical flows, visible focus states, readable contrast where practical, adequate mobile touch targets, reduced-motion consideration for nonessential animation, and color-blind-safe state cues for gameplay outcomes.
